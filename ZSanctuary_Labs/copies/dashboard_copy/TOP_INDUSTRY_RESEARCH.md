# TOP INDUSTRY RESEARCH · Z LAB

## AI Genius Compliance (EU + global)

- The EU AI Act entered into force on 1 August 2024, the first tranche of prohibitions and literacy obligations applies from 2 February 2025, and the remaining transparency/high-risk rules (Annex III) must be enforced by 2 August 2026 (with rollover items into 2027).  
  Source: [EU AI Act policy overview](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai) and the [AI Act implementation timeline](https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/eu-ai-act-implementation-timeline).
- High-risk providers must document risk assessments, data governance, third-party conformity certification, logging for traceability, human oversight schemes, and transparency disclosures for both high-risk and general-purpose AI deployments.  
  Source: [AI Act implementation materials / Digital Omnibus guide](https://www.aiacto.eu/en/echeancier-ai-act).

## Science Excellence (FAIR, GA4GH, stewardship)

- FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) continue to be the minimum requirement for research data and metadata, insisting on persistent identifiers, rich/authorized metadata, standardized vocabularies, provenance logs, and machine-actionable delivery by default.  
  Source: [NIST FAIR-Data Principles](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ssd/information-systems-group/configurable-data-curation-system-cdcs/cdcs-help-and-resources-1).
- GA4GH’s Framework for Responsible Sharing of Genomic and Health-Related Data embeds human rights, privacy, and accountability as foundational principles and core elements for interoperability, consent, ethical governance, engagement, and proportional data sharing.  
  Source: [GA4GH Framework for responsible sharing](https://www.ga4gh.org/framework/).

## Medical Intelligence / Digital Health (FDA + oversight)

- FDA’s Digital Health Policy Navigator is the central tool for determining whether a software function is a medical device, routing teams through seven steps (medical purpose, administrative support, healthy-lifestyle, records, data transfer, clinical decision support, existing guidances) to establish enforcement discretion vs. active oversight.  
  Source: [Digital Health Policy Navigator](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/digital-health-center-excellence/digital-health-policy-navigator).
- December 2025 updates now allow well-validated real-world evidence (RWE) without always submitting identifiable patient-level datasets, stressing data quality, provenance, and post-market surveillance aligned with ISO 13485/CAPA; FDA finalized the [Use of Real-World Evidence to Support Regulatory Decision-Making for Medical Devices](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/use-real-world-evidence-support-regulatory-decision-making-medical-devices) guidance simultaneously.  
  Source: [Jones Day 2026 digital health law update](https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2026/02/newsletter-vital-signs-digital-health-law-update--winter-2026) and the FDA RWE guidance above.
