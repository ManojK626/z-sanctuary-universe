Z-Sanctuary Legal & Consent Center

This page keeps a clear record of how Z-Sanctuary uses automation, AI, and data across all services. It is written in plain language but designed to meet high-standard business practice.

Member Account & Sign-In

Z-Sanctuary identifies a member by their verified account (local login, workspace identity, or other configured identity provider). All project actions, dashboards, and rituals are linked back to that member identity for audit and safety.

  • Every sign-in event is logged with time, location context, and device fingerprint.
  • Suspicious or impossible sign-in patterns trigger Security Sentinel review.
  • Members can request a summary of recent sign-ins and active sessions at any time.

Service Terms & Fair Use

By using Z-Sanctuary dashboards and automated services, the member agrees to act within fair-use boundaries: no abuse, no fraud, no misuse of AI outputs, and no re-sale of system behaviors without a written agreement.

  • All automation is assistive; the member remains responsible for final decisions.
  • Critical financial or legal actions must pass an explicit consent step.
  • System health and safety rules can temporarily override automation to protect the member.

Privacy & Data Use

Z-Sanctuary treats all member inputs (text, configurations, rituals, metrics) as private by default. Data is used to run the services, improve reliability, and personalize guidance, never to build external profiles or sell to third parties.

  • Audit logs store minimal necessary context for safety and troubleshooting.
  • Where external AI services are used, only scoped, necessary snippets are sent.
  • Members can request redaction or export of their personal data as supported by law.
